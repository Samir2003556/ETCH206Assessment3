Introduction

Infants and toddlers experience rapid growth across all developmental areas from birth to age three. Have you ever thought about how much learning happens in those early years? This stage shapes lifelong health, learning, and adaptability (DEEWR, 2009). Development is interconnected, so progress in one area boosts others.

Jean Piaget’s sensorimotor stage shows how children learn through senses and actions, building problem-solving skills (Berk, 2023). What would you do to guide a child exploring their world? Vygotsky’s Zone of Proximal Development highlights how adult support extends learning (Arthur et al., 2023). Bronfenbrenner reminds us that family, community, and culture all influence growth, making inclusive environments essential.

Next, the Early Years Learning Framework (EYLF V2.0) focuses on belonging, being, and becoming through play-based, culturally responsive practices (Australian Government Department of Education, 2022). Finally, it links with the National Quality Framework to ensure children’s wellbeing and high-quality care (ACECQA, 2022).
