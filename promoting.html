Promoting Development, Learning & Wellbeing

Promoting infant and toddler development starts with secure relationships. Have you ever noticed how eye contact or a gentle smile can make a child feel safe? Consistent, warm responses build trust and emotional security (Bowlby, 1988). Next, think about communication. What would you do if a baby babbled to you? Responding with “serve and return” interactions supports language and identity (EYLF V2.0 Outcome 1).

After that, safe and stimulating environments matter. How do you feel about using natural, familiar materials for children to explore (Arthur et al., 2023)? Play-based learning follows their curiosity, letting creativity and problem-solving grow (Pendergast & Garvis, 2023).

Finally, observing all areas of development helps plan for each child’s needs while calm spaces and gentle voices teach emotional regulation (Berk & Meyers, 2023). Have you thought about how your reactions shape a child’s sense of belonging? Small, mindful actions create lasting wellbeing.
