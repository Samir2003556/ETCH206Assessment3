Contemporary Pedagogy & Teaching Strategies

Have you ever thought about how infants and toddlers learn? Teaching this age group isn’t about lessons or worksheets – it’s about relationships, play, and everyday interactions. When a toddler points at a toy or a baby smile back at you, those are teaching moments. Educators use responsive, child-led strategies that focus on following the child’s cues and interests (Kaywork, 2020). This approach builds trust and supports all areas of development.

Next, strong, nurturing relationships are at the heart of good practice. Research indicates that when babies feel safe and connected, their brains develop in remarkable ways (Harding, 2023). The Early Years Learning Framework (EYLF) highlights play-based learning and secure attachments from birth. After that, educators’ model positive language, sing songs, and engage in play to help infants explore their world (Garvis et al., 2019). Theories like attachment and socio-cultural theory remind us that children thrive when learning happens in warm, supportive environments. How do you feel knowing that your smile, your voice, and your care are the most powerful teaching tools you have? Every cuddle, song, and peek-a-boo game builds a child’s confidence and love for learning (Sharma et al., 2021).

Teaching Competencies & Skills

Curriculum Area Competencies & Skills
Art Provide sensory experiences with safe materials; encourage exploration of colours and textures; describe children’s actions to build vocabulary.
Drama & Puppetry Use simple stories and puppets to model emotions; invite children to imitate sounds and gestures; allow children to control puppets.
Movement & Music Sing and dance daily; provide instruments and allow free movement; follow children’s rhythms and model simple actions.
Language & Literacy Read aloud and talk about pictures; use gesture and sign language; repeat and expand on children’s vocalisations.
Science Set up sensory bins and cause‑and‑effect toys; support curiosity by asking “What happens if…?” and describing outcomes.
Engineering & Technologies Offer simple construction toys; encourage problem solving; demonstrate using tools and allow children to try.
Mathematics & Numeracy Count objects during play; use comparative language (big/small, more/less); sing counting songs.
Humanities & Social Sciences Share family stories and cultural traditions; display diverse family photographs; encourage empathy through role play.
Integrated Curriculum (STEM/STEAM) Combine art and engineering in building projects; explore nature; integrate songs and movement into science experiments.

Scaffolding Across Age Groups

Infants and toddlers require different levels of support. Younger babies rely heavily on adults to model and guide interactions, while toddlers begin to take more initiative. This bar chart illustrates how the amount of adult scaffolding typically decreases as children’s autonomy increases with age.

Example Strategies by Age

Age Group Teaching Strategy Description
0–12 months Imitate & narrate Follow the infant’s focus of attention. Imitate vocalisations and gestures, describe what the child is doing and provide emotional warmth.
12–24 months Offer choices Provide two or three materials and let the toddler choose. Scaffold language by naming objects and actions; expand on their words.
2–3 years Problem solving & questions Pose simple problems (e.g., “How can we reach that toy?”). Ask open‑ended questions and encourage collaboration with peers.
