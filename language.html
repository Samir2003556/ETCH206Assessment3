Facilitating Language & Literacy Development

Language and literacy development describes the early skills upon which infants and toddlers can reach the level of communication, understanding, and reading and writing texts. It comprises words and phrases, non-verbal and verbal interaction, vocabulary building, phonological awareness, and emergent literacy (Neuman & Dickinson, 2021). The use of cry, coo and babble as the basis of language development in infants and toddlers is followed in their language development by the use of first words, two-word combinations or sentence-like structures. The process of literacy development is achieved through exposure to books, print awareness and realisation that symbols have a meaning (Roskos et al., 2023).

Teaching Competencies & Skills

Teaching Competencies

Engaging in back-and-forth interactions, following children's interests and cues. Using varied vocabulary, descriptive language, and complex sentence structures. Establishing print-rich environments with accessible books and writing materials. Incorporating rhymes, songs, and sound play into daily routines. Providing appropriate levels of support to extend children's communication attempts. Honouring home languages and incorporating diverse literary traditions.

Curriculum Areas Focused

  • Art: Provides opportunities for painting and drawing, supporting self-expression and communication.
  • Music: Combines lyrics and melody to offer infants an emotional outlet and language exposure.

Picture Books

  • Once Upon a Penguin – Uses simple, repeated phrases like “fluffy” and “cold” to support vocabulary acquisition and provide rich language experiences (Department of Education, Employment and Workplace Relations, 2009).
  • How to Count to One (and don’t even think about bigger numbers!) – Supports phonological and number recognition, foundational for early reading and writing (Department of Education, Employment and Workplace Relations, 2009).

Songs

  • Old MacDonald Had a Farm – Supports phonological awareness and animal sound recognition.
  • Hello, Hello, What’s Your Name? – Encourages verbal participation and action vocabulary.

Rhymes & Lullabies

  • Five Little Ducks – Strengthens sequencing, storytelling, number concepts, and rhyme.
  • Rock-a-Bye Bear – Soothing lullaby promoting emotional regulation and bonding.

Movement Game

Story Action Adventures: Children act out familiar stories using movements and gestures while educators narrate, promoting vocabulary comprehension, narrative understanding, and gross motor development through embodied storytelling experiences that strengthen language-movement connections.
